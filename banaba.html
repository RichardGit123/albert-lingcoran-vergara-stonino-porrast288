Banaba
(Lagerstroemia speciosa)

About Banaba

Banaba, also known as the "Queen's Flower" or "Pride of India," is a medicinal tree native to the Philippines and widely cultivated in Bukidnon. Its leaves have been used in traditional medicine for centuries to treat diabetes and kidney problems. Banaba contains corosolic acid, a compound scientifically proven to lower blood sugar levels and improve insulin sensitivity.

Medicinal Uses

  • Blood Sugar Control: effectively lowers blood glucose levels in people with Type 2 diabetes and prediabetes.
  • Weight Management: supports healthy weight loss by regulating metabolism and reducing fat accumulation.
  • Cholesterol Reduction: helps lower LDL cholesterol and triglyceride levels.
  • Kidney Health: traditionally used to treat kidney disorders and urinary problems.
  • Antioxidant Properties: protects cells from oxidative stress and free radical damage.

Preparation & Dosage

Banaba Tea:

  • Fresh Leaves: Boil 5-10 fresh banaba leaves in 2-3 cups of water for 10-15 minutes.
  • Dried Leaves: Boil 5-10 dried leaves in 2-3 cups of water for 10-15 minutes. Let steep, then strain.
  • Dosage: Drink 1 cup, 2-3 times daily before meals for best results.

Capsules/Tablets: Standardized banaba extract capsules containing 1% corosolic acid are available. Typical dosage is 32-48 mg daily.

Caution: Monitor blood sugar levels closely if you are taking diabetes medications, as banaba may enhance their effects.
