Tsaang Gubat
(Ehretia microphylla)

About Tsaang Gubat

Tsaang Gubat, also known as "Wild Tea," is a shrub that grows abundantly in Bukidnon's forests and open areas. It is one of the 10 herbal medicines endorsed by the Philippine Department of Health (DOH) for treating stomach disorders. The leaves contain polyphenols and flavonoids with strong antimicrobial and anti-diarrheal properties.

Medicinal Uses

  • Diarrhea Relief: Effectively stops diarrhea and stomach upset.
  • Antimicrobial: Fights bacterial infections in the digestive system.
  • Oral Health: Used as a mouthwash to treat mouth sores and gum infections.

Preparation & Dosage

For Diarrhea: Boil 5-10 fresh or dried leaves in 2 cups of water for 15 minutes. Let cool and strain.
Dosage: Drink 1/2 cup every 3-4 hours until symptoms subside.
For Mouthwash: Use the cooled decoction as a gargle 3 times daily.
