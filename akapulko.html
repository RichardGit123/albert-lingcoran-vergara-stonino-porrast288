Akapulko
(Cassia alata)

About Akapulko Plant

Akapulko, also known as "Ringworm Bush" is widely abundant in Bukidnon and endorsed by the Philippine Department of Health (DOH). Traditionally, it is the primary natural remedy for treating various fungal skin infections and is often used in local communities as an antifungal lotion or soap.

Medicinal Uses

  • Treats fungal infections like ringworm (buni), eczema, and scabies
  • Used as a natural antiseptic for skin healing
  • Leaves possess strong antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties

Preparation & Dosage

Fresh leaves are pounded or crushed to extract the juice, which is applied directly to the affected skin area 1-2 times a day. For stronger potency, the leaves can be boiled and the decoction used as a wound wash.
