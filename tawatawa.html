Tawa-Tawa
(Euphorbia hirta)

About Tawa-Tawa

Tawa-Tawa, also known as "Gatas-Gatas," is a hairy herb found in open grasslands, roadsides, and pathways throughout Bukidnon and the Philippines. It has gained widespread recognition for its traditional use in treating dengue fever. Scientific studies have shown that Tawa-Tawa can significantly increase platelet counts, making it a valuable supportive therapy for dengue patients experiencing thrombocytopenia (low platelet count).

Medicinal Uses

  • Dengue Support: increases platelet count in dengue patients and reduces fever symptoms.
  • Anti-inflammatory: reduces inflammation and pain associated with various conditions.
  • Respiratory Relief: treats asthma, bronchitis, and cough.
  • Digestive Aid: used for diarrhea, dysentery, and gastritis.
  • Antimicrobial: has antibacterial, antiviral, and antifungal properties.

Preparation & Dosage

Tawa-Tawa Decoction for Dengue:

  • Using Whole Plant: Remove roots, wash thoroughly. Boil in 3-5 cups of water for 5 minutes. Let cool before drinking.
  • Using Leaves Only: Take a handful of leaves, wash, and boil in 1 cup of water for 3 minutes or until the water turns blackish-green. Let cool before drinking.
  • Dosage: Drink 1-2 cups per day for 2-3 days or as needed during dengue recovery.

Capsule Form: Standardized Tawa-Tawa capsules are available. Take 1-2 capsules, 3 times daily before meals (up to 6 capsules per day for a 60kg adult).

Important Note: Tawa-Tawa should be used as a supportive therapy alongside proper medical treatment for dengue. Always consult a healthcare provider and monitor platelet counts regularly.
