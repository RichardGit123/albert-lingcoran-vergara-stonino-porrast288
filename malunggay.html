Malunggay
(Moringa oleifera)

About Malunggay

Malunggay, also known as the "Miracle Tree" or "Drumstick Tree," is one of the most nutrient-dense plants in the world. Widely cultivated in Bukidnon and throughout the Philippines, every part of the tree—from leaves to roots—has medicinal and nutritional value. The leaves are exceptionally rich in vitamins A, C, calcium, iron, and protein, often surpassing common foods like carrots, oranges, and milk.

Medicinal Uses

  • Nutritional Supplement: combats malnutrition with high levels of vitamins, minerals, and protein.
  • Anti-inflammatory & Antioxidant: protects cells from damage and reduces inflammation.
  • Blood Sugar Management: helps lower blood glucose levels in people with diabetes.
  • Cardiovascular Health: supports heart health by lowering cholesterol and blood pressure.
  • Lactation Support: traditionally used to increase breast milk production in nursing mothers.
  • Immune Booster: strengthens the body's defense against infections.

Preparation & Dosage

Malunggay Tea:

  • Fresh Leaves: Boil 1 cup of fresh leaves in 2 cups of water for 5-10 minutes.
  • Dried Leaves: Add 1 tablespoon of dried malunggay powder to 1 cup of hot water. Let steep for 3-5 minutes.
  • Dosage: Drink 1-2 cups daily for general wellness.

As Food: Fresh malunggay leaves can be added to soups (tinola), stir-fries, and salads for a nutritional boost.

Note: Pregnant women should consult a healthcare provider before consuming large amounts, as some parts of the plant may have abortifacient properties.
