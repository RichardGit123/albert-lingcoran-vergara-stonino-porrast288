Turmeric
(Curcuma longa)

About Turmeric

Known locally as "Luyang Dilaw" or "Dulaw," Turmeric is widely cultivated in the rich soils of Bukidnon, particularly in Talakag and Lantapan. It is a staple in traditional medicine for its powerful anti-inflammatory and antioxidant compound called curcumin.

Medicinal Uses

  • Anti-inflammatory: effective in reducing arthritis pain and joint swelling.
  • Digestive Aid: helps relieve bloating, gas, and stomach acidity.
  • Liver Detox: supports liver function and helps flush out toxins.
  • Skin Health: traditional remedy for minor cuts, bruises, and skin infections due to antimicrobial properties.

Preparation & Dosage

Turmeric Tea (Salabat): Wash and peel a thumb-sized piece of fresh turmeric rhizome.

  • Boiling: Slice thinly and boil in 2 cups of water for 10-15 minutes until the water turns golden yellow.
  • Enhancement: Add honey or calamansi to taste. Adding a pinch of black pepper significantly increases the absorption of curcumin.
  • Dosage: Drink 1-2 cups daily for general wellness.
