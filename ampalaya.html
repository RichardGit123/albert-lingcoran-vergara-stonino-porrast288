Ampalaya
(Momordica charantia)

About Ampalaya

Ampalaya, also known as bitter gourd or bitter melon, is one of the most recognized medicinal plants in the Philippines. The Department of Health (DOH) has approved it as a herbal medicine for blood sugar management. Despite its bitter taste, it has been used for generations in Bukidnon and throughout the country as a natural remedy for diabetes.

Medicinal Uses

  • Blood Sugar Regulation: helps lower blood glucose levels in people with Type 2 diabetes.
  • Digestive Aid: promotes healthy digestion and relieves constipation.
  • Immune Support: rich in vitamin C and antioxidants that strengthen the immune system.
  • Cholesterol Management: may help reduce bad cholesterol (LDL) levels.

Preparation & Dosage

Ampalaya Tea:

  • Using Leaves: Boil 6-8 fresh leaves in 2 cups of water for 10-15 minutes. Strain and let cool.
  • Using Fruit: Slice one medium-sized ampalaya into thin pieces. Boil in 2 cups of water for 15 minutes.
  • Dosage: Drink 1/3 cup (around 50ml) 3 times a day, after meals.

Caution: Ampalaya should not be used by pregnant women. Consult with a healthcare provider before using as a diabetes treatment, especially if taking medications.
