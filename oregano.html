Oregano
(Origanum vulgare)

About Oregano

Oregano is a popular medicinal herb in Bukidnon and throughout the Philippines, valued for its powerful antibacterial and antioxidant properties. Traditionally used to relieve respiratory ailments, oregano has been recognized for treating coughs, colds, asthma, and bronchitis. It is also effective for digestive disorders and skin conditions.

Medicinal Uses

  • Respiratory Relief: treats chronic coughs, bronchitis, asthma, and common colds.
  • Antibacterial & Antiviral: helps fight infections and supports immune function.
  • Digestive Aid: relieves indigestion, upset stomach, and bloating.
  • Anti-inflammatory: reduces pain and swelling associated with rheumatism and arthritis.
  • Wound Healing: applied topically to treat insect bites, wounds, and stings.

Preparation & Dosage

Oregano Tea for Colds & Cough:

  • Fresh Leaves: Boil 1 cup of fresh oregano leaves in 3 cups of water for 10-15 minutes.
  • Dried Leaves: Boil 2 tablespoons of dried oregano in 1 pint (2 cups) of water for 10 minutes.
  • Dosage: Drink half a cup, 3 times a day after meals.

For Chronic Coughs: Extract juice from fresh oregano leaves and take 1 tablespoon every hour.

For Wounds & Insect Bites: Apply crushed fresh leaves directly to the affected area as a poultice.
